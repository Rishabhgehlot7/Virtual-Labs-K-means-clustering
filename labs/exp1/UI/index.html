Instructions

  1. Click on the CREATE.
  2. Enter the values of x and y in the data set.
  3. Click on the ADD button.
  4. Repeat steps 2 and 3 up to ten times.
  5. Click on the NEXT button.
  6. Select the value of Clusters, click on the SUBMIT button, and then click on the NEXT button.
  7. Select centroids and click on the SUBMIT button, then click on the NEXT button.
  8. Find out the Euclidean distance between a centroid and a data point by clicking on the buttons below the table.
  9. Now click on the highlighted cell (K) and enter the cluster value (Hint: provide the index of the MIN(C1,C2..CN), the index starts from 1).
  10. Repeat step 9 for all cells in the CLUSTER column.
  11. Click on the CENTROIDS button and compute the new centroid of each Cluster.
  12. Repeat steps 8 to 11 until no data points change Clusters.
  13. If the Cluster matches the new Cluster, then click on the NEXT button to change the COMPUTE tab to the ANALYSE tab.
  14. Click on the PLOT button to show the graph.
